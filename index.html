ACTION INK.

Turn Volunteering into Real-world IT Solutions

About Us

Action Ink. is an innovative IT consultancy supporting NGOs with limited resources in creating and managing critical IT projects. By linking qualified university student volunteers to well-defined projects at international organisations, Action Ink. supports both student’s professional development and the delivery of projects that would have otherwise been impossible to deliver.

Student Volunteers

A select group of post-graduate students volunteer their time during semesters at Action Ink., supported and managed by the experienced team at Action Lab. Students gain valuable experience working in real-world IT projects for international NGOs, whilst producing concrete examples of work for their CV.

Sustainable IT Projects

Action Ink. works with organisations to design projects that map to the skills and experience of students and the capacity of the team, to deliver complete and sustainable IT projects.

Our Partners

We are proud to be partnering with the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent (through Solferino Academy) to support National RC societies across the world.

How We Work

Action Ink. utilizes a "Burst" mode to ensure the successful delivery of infrastructures and solutions. Successful candidates initially join the 10-week Development Burst in either Semester 1 or Semester 2 from Week 0 to 10 to intensively work on well-scoped projects for NGOs. Documented and implemented projects will be signed-off and handed over to NGOs at the end of each Development Burst.

The Design and Scoping Bursts come in between the Development Bursts, which involves setting and prioritizing goals, defining major deliverables, creating project schedules and milestones, and finalizing the project plan and agreements between the NGO partners. More experienced Action Ink. members will also have the chance to contribute to the Design and Scoping Burst after accomplishing at least two Development Bursts.

Who We Are?

Action Ink's services are delivered by the operational team under the governance of the Accountability Board. Leading by the Executive Board, multiple delivery leads manages the operations of IT solutions with volunteered-based project teams forming by 3 to 5 postgraduate students.

Each project team consists of developers, designers, and project managers depending on the project scope.

The Accountability Board reviews Action Ink.'s operations through a semi-year meeting to ensure the transparency and accountability of Action Ink.'s works.

Who Should Apply

Eligibility

Are you a newly enrolled student who is panic about what is your future roadmap in the next couple of years? Are you a current student who is looking for opportunities to build up your employment competencies? Have you recently graduated and willing to gain more real-world working experiences? If you answered yes to any of these questions, you are the right person we are looking for. However, to be an eligible candidate for Action Ink., you should:

  • Have the potential to apply all-round communication skills to work collaboratively within the solution delivery team
  • Striving for solving real-world problems and providing workable solutions by actively seeking out new knowledge
  • And most importantly, have a passion for digital technologies and believe they can make an impact on the world

Availability

You need to commit at least 10 hours per week from Week 0 to Week 10 in each semester to successfully participate in the project. Students can work individually and remotely for most of the time during the week, but certain core hours will be agreed upon for regularly planning and catch-up meetings.

How to Get Involved?

Accountability Board

At Action Ink. we aim to be a transparent and accountable initiative, recognising that our primary responsibility is to both the organisations we work with and the students that work within Action Ink.

To maintain such accountability, we have an Accountability Board who meet twice a year to review Action Ink.’s:
  • Current project portfolio
  • Working practices
  • Student wellbeing and support
  • External organisational relationships
This team comprises members from across the university and NGO sector, whose primary responsibility is to the students and organisations we work with.

This team receives a summary report from the Action Ink. leadership team prior to each meeting, and can propose recommendations or suggestions for improvement as appropriate. If you have any concerns or questions related Action Ink., please feel free to contact the Accountability Board for more information.
