ACTION INK.

Turn Volunteering into Real-world IT Solutions

About Us

Action Ink. is an innovative IT consultancy supporting NGOs with limited resources in creating and managing critical IT projects. By linking qualified university student volunteers to well defined projects at international organisations, Action Ink. supports both student’s professional development and the delivery of projects that would have otherwise been impossible to deliver.

Student Volunteers

A select group of post-graduate students volunteer their time during semester at Action Ink., supported and managed by the experienced team at Action Lab. Students gain valuable experience working in real-world IT projects for international NGOs, whilst producing concrete examples of work for their CV.

Sustainable IT Projects

Action Ink. works with organisations to design projects that map to the skills and experience of students and capacity of the team, to deliver complete and sustainable IT projects.

Our Partners

We are proud to be partnering with the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent (through Solferino Academy) to support National RC societies across the world.

Who We Are?

Action Ink's services are delivered by the operational team under the governance of the oversight team. Leading by the executive team, multiple delivery leads manages the operations of IT solutions with volunteered-based project teams forming by 4 to 5 postgraduate students.

Each project team is consist of a number of developers, designers, and one project manager depending on the project scope.

The oversight team reviews Action Ink.'s operations through a semi-year meeting to ensure the transparency and accountability of Action Ink.'s works.

For Students

Action Ink. provides opportunities for students to work on real-world projects on a voluntary basis to gain valuable experiences and build up their CV during their postgradute study. Suitable candidates form a Agile project team with 4 to 5 members to work collaboratively on a well-defined project under the guidance of the leadership team of Action Ink.

What to Expect

Real-world Project Experiences

Students will be given opportunities to work on real projects with world's largest humanitarian organisation. Dedicated working space at Action Lab in Clayton Campus will aslo be provided.

Recommendation Letters

Successful candicates will be awarded recommendation letters from both Monash University and the National Red Cross Society they work with.

Build up Competencies

Group of domain experts will legitimately sign-off on students competencies accumulated during the projects.

Opportunies for Trainings and Certification

Depending on the project scope, students will be provided with proper training and cloud certification opportunities when necessary.

Who Should Apply

Eligibility

To be eligible for the Action Ink. program, students must meet the following criteria:

  • Has successfully completed 48 points of FIT units (including level 9 foundation units)
  • Achieved an overall average of at least 75 percent across all of these units
  • Has outstanding all-round communication skills and ability to work collaboratively under project scopes
  • And most importantly, demonstrates a passion for solving IT problems and providing workable solutions by actively seeking out new knowledge!

Availability

You need to commit at least 10 hours per week from Week 0 to Week 10 in each semester to successfully participate in the project. Students can work separately for most of the time during the week, but certain core hours will be agreed for the team members to meet and work together.

How to Apply

If you meet all the criteria mentioned above, congratulations, you are ready to apply to join Action Ink. now! Action Ink. launches the recruitment process one month before the start of each semester. To apply for joining, you will need to fill out an online application form by:

  • Reading and following all the instructions
  • Providing your personal details
  • Uploading your resume
  • Answering the open-ended questions by complying strictly with the word or character limits for your answers
  • Providing a link to the GitHub repository for your tech assessment

Applications are now open for the S1 2021 intake. Please submit your application via Action Ink. Application Form before 14th Feb, 2021 to join us!

Oversight Team

At Action Ink. we aim to be a transparent and accountable initiative, recognising that our primary responsibility is to both the organisations we work with, and the students that work within Action Ink.

To maintain such accountability, we have an oversight team who meet twice a year to review Action Ink.’s:
  • Current project portfolio
  • Working practices
  • Student wellbeing and support
  • External organisational relationships
This team comprises members from across the university and NGO sector, whose primary responsibility is to the students and organisations we work with.

This team receives a summary report from the Action Ink. leadership team prior to each meeting, and can propose recommendations or suggestions for improvement as appropriate.
